You just clicked a suspicious link

In a real attack, this page could have stolen your credentials, installed malware, or hijacked your session. You are safe — but this is exactly how phishing works.

🛡 How to protect yourself

  • Never click links from unknown senders
    Attackers send messages that look legitimate — from banks, HR, IT support, or colleagues. Always verify the sender directly.
  • Hover before you click
    Always hover over a link to preview the real URL in the browser status bar. If the destination looks strange — don't click.
  • Check for HTTPS — but don't trust it blindly
    The padlock icon means the connection is encrypted, NOT that the site is legitimate. Phishing sites use HTTPS too.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
    Even if attackers steal your password, MFA stops them from logging in. Enable it on every critical account.
